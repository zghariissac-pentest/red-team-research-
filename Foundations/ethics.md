## legal-ethics.md

Legal and ethical considerations form the foundation of every red team operation. Offensive security work involves actions that would be illegal without proper authorization. A professional red team must operate within strict boundaries to ensure safety, legality, and client trust.

## Legal Authorization

Every engagement must be backed by written, signed authorization. This includes the scope of the test, allowed techniques, restricted assets, time windows, and emergency contacts. Authorization protects both the client and the operators from legal and operational risk.

Without explicit permission, any offensive action is illegal. Red teamers must never perform testing outside the defined scope or reuse client infrastructure, data, or payloads for other purposes. Authorization is not a formality. It is a critical layer of protection.

## Defined Scope

The scope describes what is allowed and what is prohibited. It includes domains, IP ranges, cloud accounts, user groups, and systems that are in or out of scope. A clear scope avoids accidental impact on systems that the client does not own or cannot risk testing.

Red teams must follow the scope strictly. If something falls in the gray area, it must be clarified before action. Scope drift leads to legal issues and can damage trust.

## Ethical Conduct

Red teaming involves simulating malicious behavior, but the intent and execution must remain ethical. Operators must avoid unnecessary damage, respect user privacy, and limit data collection to what is required for mission objectives.

Ethical red teamers do not harm production environments. They do not access sensitive data unless it is required to prove impact and explicitly allowed. They do not disrupt business operations or manipulate real users beyond the agreed engagement design.

The purpose is to help organizations improve, not to exploit their weaknesses.

## Safety and Risk Management

Offensive actions introduce technical risk. Payloads can cause instability, automated tools can behave unpredictably, and misconfigured infrastructure can expose sensitive information. A professional red team manages these risks through careful planning, controlled execution, and continuous monitoring.

Operators must understand the technical impact of every tool and technique before using it. High risk actions must be avoided unless explicitly approved. Safety is as important as realism.

## Privacy Protection

Red team operations often involve interacting with employees, internal systems, and sensitive data. Privacy must be maintained at all times. Only collect what is absolutely necessary to demonstrate risk. Do not store personal data longer than required, and never use it for purposes outside the engagement.

Respect for privacy protects individuals and reduces the legal burden on the client.

## Reporting Transparency

Ethical behavior extends to the final report. Findings must be accurate, evidence based, and presented without exaggeration. A truthful report helps the organization strengthen its defenses and reflects the professionalism of the red team.

Hiding mistakes or omitting relevant events damages credibility and violates ethical standards.

## Conclusion

Legal and ethical discipline is what separates professional red teamers from criminals. Authorization, scope control, privacy protection, and transparent reporting ensure the engagement delivers real value without creating harm.
